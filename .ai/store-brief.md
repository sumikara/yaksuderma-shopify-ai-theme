# Store Brief — YaksuDerma

## 1. Purpose

This file explains how the YaksuDerma Shopify store should function as an e-commerce experience.

Codex should use this file to understand:

- The business model
- The customer experience
- The trust strategy
- The pricing strategy
- The homepage purpose
- The product discovery flow
- The retail vs wholesale balance
- The UX priorities for Shopify theme development

This file should be read together with:

- `.ai/codex-instructions.md`
- `.ai/brand-brief.md`
- `.ai/catalog-brief.md`
- `.ai/navigation-brief.md`
- `.ai/design-system.md`
- `.ai/content-rules.md`

## 2. Store Type

YaksuDerma is primarily a **B2C-style Shopify e-commerce store**.

The store should feel retail-friendly, easy to browse, and approachable for individual customers.

Wholesale, clinic, and business inquiries are allowed, but they are secondary.

The website should not feel like:

- A cold distributor portal
- A complex B2B catalog
- A generic marketplace
- A suspicious discount store
- A medical claim-heavy website

The website should feel like:

- A trusted Korean-origin aesthetics product store
- A clean professional e-commerce experience
- A warm and helpful customer-first store
- A fair-price alternative to overpriced competitors
- A product discovery platform with organized categories

## 3. Business Model

Primary business model:

- Retail-first e-commerce
- B2C product browsing and purchasing
- Clear collection-based product discovery
- Trust-building product presentation
- Accessible pricing
- Helpful customer communication

Secondary business model:

- Wholesale inquiry
- Clinic inquiry
- Professional buyer inquiry
- Bulk order discussion
- Product availability questions

Wholesale should be visible but subtle.

Preferred wholesale placement:

- Footer link
- Contact page section
- Small homepage support section
- Product page inquiry link
- Optional “Business inquiry” CTA

Wholesale should not dominate the homepage.

## 4. Market Strategy

YaksuDerma’s early-stage strategy is to gain customers by offering Korean-origin products at more accessible prices than many competitors.

The store should communicate fair pricing without looking cheap.

Important positioning:

- Fair pricing
- Original products
- Korean-origin product focus
- Warm customer relationship
- Helpful support
- Professional product organization
- Trust-first presentation
- Long-term customer loyalty

Avoid:

- “Cheap” positioning
- Aggressive discount design
- Marketplace-style sale pressure
- Flash-sale aesthetics
- Countdown timer pressure
- Overuse of red sale badges
- Claims like “lowest price guaranteed” unless explicitly verified

## 5. Target Markets

Primary region:

- Europe

Secondary region:

- Asia

Tertiary region:

- Middle East

The store should look internationally trustworthy.

Design should avoid feeling too local, amateur, or informal.

The store should be understandable to customers from different regions, especially customers who are comparing Korean-origin aesthetics products online.

## 6. Target Customers

Primary customers:

- Retail customers interested in Korean-origin aesthetics products
- Customers seeking original products at fair prices
- Customers who value trust and customer support
- Customers comparing prices across international sellers
- Customers who need clear category-based product discovery

Secondary customers:

- Clinics
- Beauty professionals
- Aesthetic professionals
- Small business buyers
- Wholesale buyers
- Professional product resellers or buyers

The store should be simple enough for retail customers but professional enough to not alienate business buyers.

## 7. Customer Psychology

Customers may worry about:

- Product originality
- Whether the store is trustworthy
- Whether low pricing means low quality
- Whether the products are actually Korean-origin
- Whether product information is accurate
- Whether they can contact someone if they need help
- Shipping and availability uncertainty
- Whether the site is professional and legitimate

The theme should reduce these concerns through:

- Clean design
- Authenticity messaging
- Clear category structure
- Professional product cards
- Trust sections
- Visible contact/support paths
- Calm color system
- Consistent typography
- Transparent wording
- No exaggerated claims

## 8. Core Store Promise

The store experience should communicate:

> Authentic Korean-origin aesthetics products, organized clearly, offered with fair pricing and warm customer support.

The customer should quickly understand:

1. What the store sells
2. Why the store can be trusted
3. Which product categories exist
4. Which products are important or popular
5. How to contact the store
6. Where to find product and shipping information
7. That prices are fair without the store feeling cheap

## 9. Main Store Goals

The Shopify theme should support these goals:

1. Build trust within the first few seconds.
2. Make product categories immediately clear.
3. Help customers browse large product groups easily.
4. Present long technical product names cleanly.
5. Keep pricing visible but not aggressive.
6. Guide users from homepage to collection to product page.
7. Support mobile shopping first.
8. Keep wholesale/business inquiry available but secondary.
9. Avoid medical, legal, or regulatory claims.
10. Make the store feel warm, helpful, and reliable.

## 10. Main Collections

The store should use a compact 6-collection architecture:

1. Botulinum Toxins
2. Dermal & Body Fillers
3. Skin Boosters & Exosomes
4. Mesotherapy & Contouring
5. Collagen Stimulators
6. Clinical Accessories & IV Therapy

Use these collections for:

- Main navigation
- Homepage category showcase
- Collection cards
- SEO-oriented category structure
- Product discovery flow

Do not create too many top-level navigation categories.

## 11. Homepage Role

The homepage is not just a visual landing page.

It must work as a trust-building and product-discovery page.

Homepage should answer:

- What does YaksuDerma sell?
- Are the products original?
- Are these Korean-origin products?
- Why are prices accessible?
- What are the main product categories?
- Which products or collections should I explore first?
- Can I contact someone for help?
- Are business/wholesale inquiries possible?

Recommended homepage section order:

1. Hero section with clear value proposition
2. Trust/authenticity bar
3. Main category showcase using the 6 collection groups
4. Featured collection or featured products
5. Why YaksuDerma / value proposition section
6. Customer support / help section
7. Optional wholesale inquiry section
8. Footer with trust, policies, contact, and navigation links

## 12. Homepage CTA Strategy

Primary CTA style:

- “Explore Products”
- “Shop Collections”
- “Browse Categories”
- “View Korean-Origin Products”

Secondary CTA style:

- “Contact Us”
- “Ask About Availability”
- “Wholesale Inquiry”
- “Need Help Choosing?”

Avoid:

- “Buy Now!!!”
- “Cheapest Products”
- “Limited Time Only”
- “Guaranteed Results”
- Any CTA that sounds aggressive or medically risky

## 13. Collection Page Role

Collection pages should help customers browse large product groups without confusion.

Collection pages should support:

- Clear collection title
- Short non-risky collection description
- Product grid
- Filtering
- Sorting
- Pagination
- Mobile-friendly browsing
- Clean product cards
- Long product names
- Product price visibility
- Optional trustworthy badges

Do not break Shopify native filtering, sorting, pagination, or product URLs.

Collection pages should feel organized and professional, not crowded.

## 14. Product Page Role

Product pages should help customers evaluate a product with trust and clarity.

Product pages should prioritize:

- Product image clarity
- Exact product name
- Dynamic Shopify price
- Variant logic if applicable
- Add-to-cart clarity
- Product description
- Authenticity/origin information if verified
- Contact/support option
- Related products
- Careful shipping/availability language
- Optional wholesale/business inquiry path

Do not break:

- Product form logic
- Variant picker
- Quantity selector
- Add-to-cart
- Dynamic checkout buttons
- Selling plan logic if present
- App blocks
- Shopify product data

## 15. Product Card Role

Product cards are very important because the catalog contains many technical product names.

Product cards should:

- Keep images clean and consistent
- Handle long names gracefully
- Show product price dynamically
- Avoid clutter
- Avoid aggressive sale design
- Use subtle badges only when truthful
- Work well in mobile two-column grids
- Make product comparison easy
- Feel trustworthy and organized

Avoid:

- Too many badges
- Heavy shadows
- Loud discount colors
- Cropped product images
- Tiny unreadable names
- Marketplace-style product listings

## 16. Pricing Strategy in UX

Prices should be visible but not visually aggressive.

The store should communicate:

- Fair price
- Accessible price
- Honest value
- Original product
- Trustworthy source

Preferred price presentation:

- Clean
- Readable
- Calm
- Not hidden
- Not oversized
- Not red-heavy

Avoid making low prices look suspicious.

Do not hardcode prices in the theme. Use Shopify dynamic product data.

## 17. Trust Strategy

Trust should be repeated gently across the store.

Trust signals can include:

- Original Korean-origin product positioning
- Authenticity-focused language
- Certificate information where verified
- Contact/support visibility
- Clear product categories
- Clean product presentation
- Professional visual system
- Customer-first tone
- Careful order handling language

Potential trust sections:

- “Original Korean-Origin Products”
- “Fair Pricing, Helpful Support”
- “Carefully Handled Orders”
- “Need Help? Contact Us”
- “Business or Wholesale Inquiry”

Do not invent:

- Certificates
- Regulatory approvals
- Country-specific legality
- Clinical results
- Supplier authorization
- Medical usage claims

## 18. Shipping and Availability UX

YaksuDerma should not overpromise shipping speed.

Preferred wording:

- “Availability may vary by product.”
- “Contact us for shipping and product details.”
- “Orders are handled carefully.”
- “We prioritize authenticity and proper handling.”

Avoid:

- “Fastest shipping”
- “Same-day delivery”
- “Guaranteed delivery”
- “Always in stock”
- Any claim that is not operationally guaranteed

Shipping information should feel transparent, not defensive.

## 19. Customer Support Strategy

Customer support is a core differentiator.

The site should make support easy to find.

Recommended support placements:

- Header or announcement bar if appropriate
- Homepage support block
- Product page support note
- Footer contact area
- Contact page
- Optional WhatsApp / email / inquiry CTA if provided

Tone:

- Warm
- Helpful
- Calm
- Trustworthy
- Not overly salesy

Example copy:

- “Need help choosing a product? Contact us.”
- “Questions about availability? Our team can help.”
- “For product or wholesale inquiries, reach out to us.”

## 20. Wholesale / B2B Inquiry Strategy

Wholesale is secondary but should be available.

Preferred UX:

- Add “Wholesale Inquiry” as a footer or contact link.
- Add a subtle support block on homepage.
- Add optional product page inquiry link.
- Do not make wholesale the primary homepage message.

Wholesale wording:

- “Wholesale or business inquiry?”
- “Contact us for product availability and order details.”
- “Business inquiries are welcome.”

Avoid making the site look like a trade-only B2B portal.

## 21. Navigation Strategy

Navigation should be compact and category-first.

Top-level navigation should prioritize:

- Home
- Shop / Collections
- Main product categories
- About
- Contact
- Optional Wholesale Inquiry

Navigation must not become crowded.

The six main collection groups should be used as the core shop structure.

Detailed navigation rules are documented in:

- `.ai/navigation-brief.md`

## 22. Content Strategy

Content should be:

- Clear
- Professional
- Warm
- Trust-building
- Non-aggressive
- Non-medical-claim-heavy
- Easy to scan
- Mobile-friendly

Avoid:

- Long dense text blocks
- Exaggerated claims
- Treatment promises
- Fear-based selling
- Fake urgency
- Overly technical explanations in homepage sections

Use short educational copy only when it helps product discovery.

## 23. Search and SEO Considerations

The store should support SEO-friendly collection discovery.

Important SEO principles:

- Use clear collection names.
- Use exact product names.
- Keep product names searchable.
- Avoid vague category names.
- Do not rewrite product names into decorative names.
- Use concise collection descriptions.
- Keep headings meaningful.

The store architecture should support the 6 main SEO-friendly collection groups.

## 24. Mobile UX Priorities

Mobile is a priority.

Mobile experience should support:

- Fast category understanding
- Easy product browsing
- Readable product titles
- Clear pricing
- Clean product images
- Tap-friendly buttons
- Simple navigation
- Easy contact/support access
- Product grid readability
- Fast path from homepage to product
- Clear category/collection navigation
- Strong product discovery
- Mobile-first shopping
- Trust and authenticity signals

Do not design desktop first and then squeeze it into mobile.

## 25. Accessibility Priorities

The theme should support:

- Readable contrast
- Clear button states
- Logical heading hierarchy
- Alt text support through Shopify image data
- Keyboard-friendly interactions where applicable
- Tap-friendly mobile targets
- Avoiding text over busy images
- Not relying only on color to communicate meaning

## 26. Content and Compliance Guardrails

Do not invent:

- Medical claims
- Treatment outcomes
- Dosage instructions
- Injection instructions
- Regulatory approvals
- Certificates
- Clinical claims
- Safety guarantees
- Product legality claims
- Country-specific import statements

When in doubt, use neutral product discovery language.

More detailed content safety rules are documented in:

- `.ai/content-rules.md`

## 27. Shopify Theme Rules

Codex must preserve:

- Shopify dynamic product data
- Product forms
- Cart logic
- Variant logic
- Search
- Filtering
- Sorting
- Pagination
- App blocks
- Theme editor compatibility

Prefer:

- Sections
- Blocks
- Dynamic sources
- Shopify settings
- Shopify Liquid objects
- Reusable snippets
- Maintainable CSS
- Small, reviewable changes

Do not hardcode product prices or product availability.

## 28. Store Experience Summary

YaksuDerma should feel like:

> A clean, trustworthy, retail-friendly Korean aesthetics e-commerce store where customers can discover original products at fair prices and receive helpful human support.

The store should not feel like:

- A cheap discount marketplace
- A cold medical distributor
- A luxury brand with no warmth
- A confusing technical catalog
- A risky medical claims website
- A generic Shopify template


- Clean product cards
- Fast collection browsing
