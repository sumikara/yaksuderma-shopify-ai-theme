# Catalog Brief — YaksuDerma

## 1. Purpose

This file documents YaksuDerma’s current product catalog, collection logic, SEO-friendly category structure, product priorities, and Shopify theme rules.

Codex should use this file to understand the store catalog, product hierarchy, navigation logic, and merchandising priorities.

Important: this file is for context and planning. Product data, prices, availability, images, variants, and product URLs must come dynamically from Shopify whenever possible.

Do not hardcode product prices, stock status, or product data into Liquid templates unless explicitly requested.

## 2. Catalog Strategy

YaksuDerma sells Korean-origin professional beauty and medical aesthetics products with a retail-first e-commerce approach.

Primary commercial strategy:

- Build customer trust.
- Emphasize product originality and Korean origin.
- Offer accessible pricing compared with expensive competitors.
- Keep the store professional but warm and helpful.
- Support B2C retail shopping first.
- Allow wholesale / clinic / business inquiries as a secondary path.

The catalog should not feel like a cheap marketplace. It should feel like a trustworthy, organized, professional Korean aesthetics store with clear category discovery.

## 3. Main Collection Structure

Use a maximum of 6 main collections to keep navigation compact, user-friendly, and SEO-oriented.

Main collections:

1. **Botulinum Toxins**
2. **Dermal & Body Fillers**
3. **Skin Boosters & Exosomes**
4. **Mesotherapy & Contouring**

- Botulinum Toxins

- Collagen Stimulators

- Excessively long navigation labels

## 11. Cross-Selling Rules

Recommended cross-selling logic:

- Liporase inj. can be recommended alongside Dermal & Body Fillers where appropriate.
- Numbing creams can be recommended as clinic accessory products where appropriate.
- Skin booster collections can be paired with related PDRN / exosome products.
- Do not create medical-use recommendations.
- Frame cross-sells as “Related products” or “Often viewed with”, not as medical instructions.

Preferred wording:

- “Related products”
- “You may also explore”
- “Often viewed with”
- “Pairs well in professional product discovery”

Avoid:

- “Required for treatment”
- “Use this with”
- “Must be used together”
- Any usage instruction unless explicitly provided and legally safe

## 12. Content Safety Rules

Codex must avoid:

- Medical claims
- Treatment promises
- Dosage instructions
- Injection instructions
- Regulatory claims
- Clinical performance claims
- Before/after claims
- Safety guarantees
- Country-specific legality claims
- Certification claims not provided by the user

Use neutral, professional e-commerce language.

## 13. Theme Design Implications

The catalog requires:

- Strong category navigation
- Clean product cards
- Easy mobile browsing
- Consistent product image ratios
- Product titles that handle long names well
- Product grids that do not feel crowded
- Clear but not aggressive pricing
- Optional badges with strict truth rules
- Trust sections that emphasize authenticity
- Wholesale inquiry path without making the site B2B-first

## 14. Notes for Codex

When redesigning the theme:

- Use this catalog brief for context.
- Do not hardcode catalog data.
- Prefer Shopify theme editor settings for homepage category/product selection.
- Keep product grids flexible.
- Assume the catalog will grow.
- Design for long product names.
- Design for variable product prices and variants.
- Keep collection navigation compact.
- Preserve Shopify filtering, sorting, pagination, product URLs, and product forms.
